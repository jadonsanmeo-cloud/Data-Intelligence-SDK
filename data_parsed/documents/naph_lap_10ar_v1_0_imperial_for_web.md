### National Audit of Pulmonary Hypertension


National Audit of Pulmonary Hypertension
Local Action Plan - 10th Annual Report

1
Pg
Standard
Recommendation
Planned action
23

95 per cent of patients receiving a
pulmonary hypertension drug therapy
(excluding patients diagnosed as
pulmonary arterial hypertension
associated with congenital heart
disease) should have a cardiac
catheterization recorded before
treatment begins.

PH centres who do not meet this
standard should review the cases who
have not undergone cardiac
catheterization before treatment and
put in place a strategy to rectify this.

We have reviewed the cases who have been documented not to
have undergone a cardiac catheter and all had, but in most
cases these were done externally and therefore the
documentation in the database was incorrect. The action plan is
to review standards quarterly and to identify discrepancies
closer to the time of entry.
35

90 per cent of patients who undergo a
pulmonary endarterectomy should wait
less than four months from their
diagnosis of chronic thromboembolic
pulmonary hypertension.

NHS England and PH centres need to
address the delay in undertaking
pulmonary endarterectomy. This
requires a joint effort since the patient
pathway is complex.

See Royal Papworth response
