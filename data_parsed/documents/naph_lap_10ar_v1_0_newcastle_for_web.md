### National Audit of Pulmonary Hypertension


National Audit of Pulmonary Hypertension
Local Action Plan - 10th Annual Report

1
Pg
Standard
Recommendation
Planned action
19

95 per cent of patients whose referral
letter was received over six months
ago should have a diagnosis recorded.

PH centres must document the clinical
diagnosis for as many patients as
possible so that they can progress to
appropriate treatment.

Freeman Hospital monitors all new patient activity and is
working to ensure that all patients have a diagnosis recorded
within the recognised time frame, and this information is
accurately represented within the database.
35

90 per cent of patients who undergo a
pulmonary endarterectomy should wait
less than four months from their
diagnosis of chronic thromboembolic
pulmonary hypertension.

NHS England and PH centres need to
address the delay in undertaking
pulmonary endarterectomy. This
requires a joint effort since the patient
pathway is complex.

See Royal Papworth response
